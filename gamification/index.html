Teaching Programming by Gamification


What is this?

This is a proof-of-concept demo of a game that aims to aid in computer programming education.

Aside from that, it is also inspired by many popular video games based around puzzle solving.

The game is part of my undergraduate dissertation project at the University of Lincoln, UK.

What is its purpose?

The game will be used in a research experiment to assess the effectiveness of computer games as an auxiliary form of computer programming education.

The research methodology involves a play session with the game's demo build (see: Instructions) and an anonymous online survey.

How do I take part in the research?

Essential information

Playing the game

Before playing the game, I highly advise you read the user manual.

You can download the game by clicking here. Extract the .zip file, open the extracted the folder, and run Gamification.exe

Completing the survey

The survey is done via the online, GDPR-compliant Qualtrics system and is approved by the University of Lincoln. It is anonymous, though if you wish to be informed of results of the study, you are able to optionally provide your email address at the end.

Once you have finished playing the game, please click here to fill out the survey.
